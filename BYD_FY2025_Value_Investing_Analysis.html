Business Summary / 业务概述

BYD is the world's largest NEV manufacturer by volume, headquartered in Shenzhen. In FY2025, BYD surpassed Tesla in global EV volumes for the first time when including all electrified powertrains. The company operates across three segments: automobiles & related products (~80.7% of revenue), mobile handset components & assembly (~19.3%), and rechargeable batteries & photovoltaics. BYD is vertically integrated across batteries (Blade Battery), semiconductors (BYD Semiconductor), and powertrains. In March 2026, BYD launched the second-generation Blade Battery with ultra-fast charging capability.

Key Changes: FY2025 vs. Prior Analysis / 年报变化 vs 之前分析

1. First Annual Profit Decline in 4 Years / 四年来首次年度利润下降
Net profit fell 19% to ¥32.6B, down from ¥40.3B in FY2024. This was the first annual decline since 2021. Q4 was particularly weak with profit down 38% YoY to ¥9.53B, well below consensus of ¥15B. Our prior analysis flagged margin pressure risk — this outcome was worse than base case but within our cautious scenario range.
2. Revenue Growth Decelerated Sharply / 收入增速大幅放缓
Revenue grew only 3.5% to ¥804B, a stark slowdown from +29% in FY2024. The domestic price war intensified throughout 2025, compressing average selling prices. H2 domestic ASP dropped to ~¥127,000/vehicle. Despite volume growth (+7.7%), pricing pressure dominated.
3. Gross Margin Compression Confirmed / 毛利率压缩确认
Overall gross margin fell from 19.4% → 17.7%. Auto segment margin fell to 20.5% from 22.3%. This validates our prior cautious stance on margin sustainability. Per-vehicle profit has thinned significantly from peak levels.
4. Overseas Breakthrough: 1M+ Exports / 海外突破：出口超100万辆
POSITIVE Overseas sales exceeded 1 million units for the first time (+151% YoY), covering 100+ countries. This was ahead of the original 800K target. Higher overseas margins partially offset domestic margin erosion. BYD targets 1.3M–1.5M overseas units in 2026.
5. R&D Spending Nearly 2× Net Profit / 研发支出近净利润2倍
R&D spending rose 17% to ¥63.4B (cumulative >¥240B), nearly double net profit. This reflects BYD's "engineer-first" culture. Key outputs: Gen-2 Blade Battery, DM-i/DM-p hybrid platforms, "God's Eye" ADAS system (installed in 1.7M vehicles). 120,000+ R&D engineers.
6. Balance Sheet Strengthened / 资产负债表改善
POSITIVE Total shareholder equity up ~30% YoY. Completed HK$43.5B accelerated share placement in early 2025 (largest in global auto in a decade). Shares outstanding increased from ~3.04B to ~9.12B (3:1 stock split in June 2025). Net cash position remains strong.
7. Competitive Landscape Intensified / 竞争格局加剧
WATCH Xiaomi, XPENG, Geely, and Leapmotor all gained ground. BYD's domestic market share growth slowed. Jan-Feb 2026 sales dipped YoY as competitors launched compelling models. The market has entered what BYD describes as a "knockout stage."
8. Valuation Context Shifted / 估值背景变化
Post-results stock traded at HK$105.8 (pre-split adjusted), down from 52-week high of HK$159.3. P/E now ~22x TTM on post-split EPS of ~¥3.57. Tesla comparison: BYD now ahead on net earnings, revenue, and automotive gross margin. BYD also beat Tesla on net margin for FY2025.

Reverse DCF — What's Priced In? / 反向DCF — 市场隐含预期

At the current H-share price of HK$105.8 and a 10% discount rate, the market is implying roughly 7–8% earnings growth over the next decade. Given that BYD's FY2025 earnings actually declined 19%, the market is clearly pricing in a recovery and return to growth. This is reasonable if overseas expansion delivers as planned, but leaves limited margin of safety if the domestic price war persists and overseas growth disappoints.

Investment Verdict — Updated / 投资结论 — 更新

HOLD / WATCHLIST — 观望/候选

Business Quality: BYD remains the world's preeminent NEV manufacturer with unmatched vertical integration and scale. The FY2025 results confirm both its dominance and the reality of intense competition. R&D spending at nearly 2× net profit signals long-term commitment to technological leadership.

Valuation: At HK$105.8 (P/E ~22x), BYD is not cheap by Graham standards. The stock prices in a solid recovery and ~8% sustained growth. Our base-case DCF puts fair value at ~HK$89, suggesting the stock is slightly overvalued at current levels. However, the bull case (12% growth, 9% DR) supports HK$152 — so upside exists if overseas expansion delivers above expectations.

Key Risks: (1) Domestic price war deepening — Citi warns domestic auto may turn unprofitable in Q1 2026; (2) Trade policy uncertainty (EU tariffs, potential US tariffs); (3) Margin compression may persist as competitors like Xiaomi and XPENG improve; (4) Massive capex cycle may strain FCF (TTM FCF is negative); (5) Gen-2 Blade Battery transition disrupting Q1 2026 sales.

Key Catalysts: (1) Overseas sales ramp to 1.5M units in 2026; (2) Gen-2 Blade Battery driving premium mix improvement; (3) Flash Charging infrastructure creating switching costs; (4) Hungary/Indonesia/Brazil factory ramp reducing tariff exposure; (5) Potential margin recovery in H2 2026 as new models mature.

What We're Watching Next / 后续关注

Q1 2026 results (expected late April) will be critical — expect sequential weakness from model transitions and Gen-2 Blade Battery rollout. The key question is whether the margin trough occurs in Q1 and recovery begins in Q2. Overseas sales monthly data (targeting 100K+/month) and gross margin trajectory are the most important leading indicators. If domestic margins stabilize while overseas mix improves, the investment thesis strengthens materially.
