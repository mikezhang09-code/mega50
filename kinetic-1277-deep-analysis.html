01
Understand the Business
理解生意

Kinetic Development Group is an Inner Mongolia coal miner operating the Dafanpu Coal Mine — a single large concession area of ~9.6 sq km in Zhunge'er Banner, Ordos City. The core business is simple: extract coal, wash it, transport it, sell it. Revenue comes overwhelmingly from thermal coal sales to domestic Chinese power plants and industrial users.

The company has been diversifying into non-core businesses: agriculture and animal husbandry (planting, feed processing, livestock breeding, food processing), real estate (office buildings, residential, parking), and tobacco/cigars (production, wholesale, e-vapor). These non-core businesses are early-stage and currently loss-making (RMB80-90M losses in FY2025), but management views them as long-term diversification away from coal dependency.

How It Makes Money 盈利模式

Coal mining and sales generate ~95% of revenue. The Dafanpu mine produces thermal coal used primarily for power generation. Revenue is directly tied to coal prices × volume. Low-cost producer due to open-pit/shallow mine structure in Inner Mongolia. Transportation via local rail to Shuozhou-Huanghua rail line.

Competitive Test 竞争力测试

What if a well-funded competitor entered? Coal mining is a resource-based business — you need the concession. Kinetic's Dafanpu mine is a fixed, depletable asset with government-granted mining rights. New competitors can't replicate the specific mine, but coal is a fungible commodity — price competition comes from other mines and imports. Kinetic's edge is cost structure, not differentiation.

Moat Verdict: Narrow — Cost-Based 窄护城河 — 成本优势型

Kinetic has a narrow moat based on cost advantage and resource access. This is typical of well-run commodity producers — the moat protects profitability at low points in the cycle but doesn't provide pricing power. The 48.7% gross margin and 37.3% net margin are unusually high for coal, confirming the low-cost position. However, the moat is depleting — as the mine's reserves are exhausted, the moat narrows unless new concessions are acquired. The non-core diversification (agriculture, real estate, tobacco) does not currently contribute to moat strength.

05
Management Quality
管理层质量

Alignment: 74% Insider Ownership 利益一致性

Founder Liang Zhang holds 63% of shares outstanding. Total insider ownership is 74%. This is exceptionally high alignment — management's net worth is deeply tied to the stock price. Buffett's "owner-operator" ideal. They eat their own cooking. The downside: concentrated control means minority shareholders have limited influence on governance decisions.

Capital Allocation 资本配置

Positive signals: Ordinary dividends were raised in FY2025 despite 50-60% profit decline — signals management confidence in the business's resilience and commitment to shareholder returns. Payout ratio 31-47%. D/E improved from 26.6% to 17.6% over 5 years (paying down debt). Concerns: Non-core diversification (agriculture, tobacco, real estate) is consuming capital with RMB80-90M losses in FY2025. Related-party loans to Guizhou Liliang and property acquisitions tied to major shareholder flagged by Simply Wall St.

🚩 Governance Red Flag: Related-Party Transactions 关联交易红旗

Simply Wall St flagged "governance heavy moves" including extending loans to Guizhou Liliang and aggregating property acquisitions tied to the major shareholder. This is a classic RF-2 (unverified cross-holdings/related-party risk) from our data discipline framework. For a company where one person controls 63%, related-party transactions are the #1 governance risk. These need detailed investigation from the annual report notes before committing capital. The question is whether these transactions are at arm's length and in minority shareholders' interest.
