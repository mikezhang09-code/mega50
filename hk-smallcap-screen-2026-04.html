筛选总结 — 共发现 8 只候选股；优质 0 只，优选 3 只，观察名单 4 只，淘汰 1 只

HK remains the richest neglect hunting ground globally — 2,600+ listings with sell-side coverage concentrated on ~80 HSI/HSCEI components. This screen surveyed 14 small/mid-cap candidates surfaced via Simply Wall St neglect filters, AASTOCKS coverage gaps, and Hong Kong dividend stock universe research, then funneled through Stage 1 (Neglect) → Stage 1.5 (Quick Verify) → Stage 2 (Quality) → Stage 3 (Valuation). Of the 8 advancing past Stage 1.5, three earned Strong Candidate status (GGB 60–79): a paper packaging name with insider buying, a building materials franchise with a wide market position, and an industrial manufacturer with a long dividend record. Four are watchlist names awaiting either earnings stabilization or governance clarity. No Prime candidates emerged — consistent with the inaugural HK screen findings (Kinetic 69.2, Dah Sing 69.7), governance discounts and cyclical timing keep HK names below the 80 threshold even when neglect and valuation are exceptional. Top 3 should proceed to full 7-step deep dive. ⚠ Tier 1 filing verification required before any capital deployment.

Known Limitations of This Screen
本次筛选已知局限

(1) FY2025 annual reports for several candidates may have been filed within last 60 days — Q1 (5-year profitability) and Q9 (going concern) need verification from the most recent filing, not Tier 2 aggregator snapshots which can lag. (2) Analyst counts vary by source by 2–8 analysts for several candidates; per Stage 1.5 protocol, the higher number was used for conservative scoring. (3) Related-party transactions (G3) cannot be assessed without reading annual report notes — all candidates show preliminary G-Score with placeholder for RPT review. (4) Cyclical positioning: cement, paper, and chemical names may be at cycle troughs where trough-PE looks expensive but normalized-PE is attractive — flag noted in candidate detail.

Per portfolio-management skill: HK small-cap neglected names start at half-position (max 3–4% portfolio weight) due to liquidity, governance, and recovery-timing uncertainty. Promote to full position only after: (1) Tier 1 verification confirms scoring, (2) two consecutive quarters of operational stability, (3) governance review confirms no material RPTs or dilution. The inaugural HK screen verdicts (Kinetic, Dah Sing) both landed at half-position recommendations for these reasons — same default applies here until proven otherwise.
