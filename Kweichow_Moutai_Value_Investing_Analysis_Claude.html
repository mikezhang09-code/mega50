The key insight / 核心观点

A 1.2% revenue decline and 4.5% profit decline on a business generating ¥169 billion at 49% net margin is a deceleration, not a collapse. For comparison, during the 2013–2014 anti-corruption shock, Moutai's revenue fell over 17%. What makes FY2025 notable is the symbolism: it ends a ~15-year streak of uninterrupted growth that had become almost a religious tenet among A-share investors.

2. Root Causes of the Decline · 业绩下滑根源

Moutai's FY2025 pain is the culmination of four compounding forces. Understanding each is essential to judge whether the slowdown is cyclical (buyable) or structural (avoidable).

Cause 1: The Second Anti-Corruption Wave (2025) · 第二轮禁酒令

In May 2025, Beijing extended alcohol restrictions — originally aimed at high-end liquor at official functions — to all alcoholic beverages at state-affiliated events. Chairman Zhang Deqin publicly endorsed it: "It's time for Chinese baijiu to return to its roots." Official consumption is now <5% of baijiu revenue (down from ~60% pre-2012), so the direct revenue hit is modest. But the signal — that ostentatious Moutai consumption is politically discouraged — depressed the broader business-gifting and banquet channels that remain Moutai's true demand base.

Cause 2: Wholesale Price Collapse from Speculative Peak · 批价从投机高位崩落

Feitian Moutai's wholesale price peaked near ¥3,400–3,850 in 2021, turning bottles into an alternative asset class. By mid-2025 the wholesale price had fallen to ~¥2,100, and by December 2025 it briefly dipped below the ¥1,499 official guide price in some channels — an unprecedented "price inversion." Distributors who had stockpiled at ¥2,000+ faced real losses. This is not a revenue problem for Moutai directly (ex-factory price is fixed), but it damaged channel confidence and forced a strategic pivot.

Cause 3: Weak China Consumer & Property-Linked Wealth Effect · 内需疲弱 · 房地产财富效应逆转

UBS research demonstrates Moutai's wholesale price has historically tracked Chinese property prices closely — reflecting its role as a store-of-value asset for wealthy Chinese households. With property in ongoing correction, the "collectible" demand for Moutai has evaporated alongside it.

Cause 4: Demographic & Taste Shift · 人口结构与口味变迁

Industry research shows 70% of young Chinese view baijiu as unhealthy and unpleasant. The primary baijiu-drinking cohort (men aged 30–59) is in structural decline. Rivals Wuliangye, Gujing, and Shede have launched low-ABV products (26–29% vs baijiu's traditional 52%+). Moutai itself now sells a blueberry sparkling wine. This is the slowest-moving but most dangerous of the four forces.

The "Great Bifurcation" / 冰火两重天

Critically, the industry top-6 (including Moutai) now capture 86% of industry profit, up from 55% in 2014. Smaller rivals like Kouzijiao expect 50–60% profit declines in 2025. Moutai's 4.5% profit decline is best-in-industry. The downturn is consolidating the market in Moutai's favor.

4. Intrinsic Value Triangulation · 内在价值三角估值

Three independent valuation methods. Single-number estimates are a trap; a range tells the story.

Method A — Graham Number (Conservative Floor) · 格雷厄姆数

Graham Number = √(22.5 × EPS × BVPS) = √(22.5 × 65.5 × 207) = ~¥552

At CNY 1,407, Moutai trades at 2.55× Graham Number. This is expected for a wide-moat consumer brand — Graham's formula intentionally penalizes quality businesses. Even Berkshire-quality names routinely trade at 2–3× Graham Number.

Method B — Owner Earnings DCF (Base Case Engine) · 所有者收益现金流折现

Starting Owner Earnings estimate: ¥80B (slightly below FY2025 net profit, reflecting that free cash flow has historically tracked net income closely given minimal maintenance capex and Moutai's working-capital tailwind from advance dealer deposits).

Method C — Dividend Discount Model Cross-Check · 股息贴现模型

With management now signaling higher payout ratios (the 2024 payout ratio reached 75%) and moving toward more market-oriented capital returns, a Gordon Growth DDM with r=9% and g=3% on a forward ¥50 dividend gives IV ≈ ¥833. This sanity-checks against the DCF and provides another anchor.

7. Risk Matrix · 风险矩阵

  • HIGH Structural demographic decline. Young Chinese don't like baijiu. This is a 10–20 year slow erosion that no management can easily fix. Moutai's brand extensions (blueberry wine, low-ABV products) are an acknowledgment, not a solution.
  • HIGH Continued policy overhang. As a Guizhou SOE (49% state-owned), Moutai is always one press release away from further restrictions. The May 2025 ban extension caught the market off-guard. Future anti-ostentation campaigns are a tail risk.
  • MEDIUM Channel transformation execution risk. The shift to direct sales via iMoutai (cutting distributors out of the premium-capture equation) is a massive channel overhaul. Distributor pushback has already emerged. If botched, revenue could fall further in 2026.
  • MEDIUM Wholesale price overhang. Wholesale price is approaching the ex-factory price. If it breaches, Moutai would face real pressure to cut ex-factory pricing, directly hitting gross margin. March 2026's ex-factory price hike was a bold move to pre-empt this.
  • MEDIUM A-share liquidity & RMB exposure. Non-Chinese investors face currency risk (RMB has been under pressure) and capital controls. Historical A-share drawdowns have exceeded 50%.
  • LOW Balance sheet risk. ~Zero debt, ¥178B net cash, 5.7× current ratio. Moutai could buy back 10% of shares in cash without strain. Bankruptcy risk is effectively zero.
  • LOW Accounting quality. Audited by Pan-China CPA; unqualified opinions. FCF has tracked reported earnings closely over 10+ years — a good sign for Chinese equity accounting.

The single most instructive historical analog is the first anti-corruption shock under Xi Jinping, which began in late 2012 and bottomed in early 2014.

Two observations: (1) Fundamentally, 2025 is far milder than 2013–14. (2) But the market also reset far less, meaning a pure mean-reversion trade is much weaker than in 2014. Anyone who bought Moutai at 8× earnings in January 2014 earned spectacular returns. At 21.5× today, the margin of safety is simply not in the same universe.

The right comparison is not "Moutai at its 2014 lows" but "Moutai at fair value with a wide moat." That's a different, more measured investment case.

9. Management's Strategic Response · 管理层战略反应

Moutai's new leadership (Chairman Zhang Deqin, CEO Chen Hua appointed late 2025) is executing an aggressive pivot. Whether it succeeds determines which of our three scenarios plays out.

Key Strategic Actions · 关键战略举措

1. Direct-to-Consumer Pivot

Jan 2026: Daily sales of ¥1,499 Feitian on iMoutai app. 70M+ users, +2.7M new users in 9 days. Collapses the scalper/distributor arbitrage margin. Signals: "Moutai is to drink, not to speculate."

2. Ex-Factory Price Hike

Mar 30, 2026: Feitian ex-factory price raised 8.6% to ¥1,269 — first hike in 2.5 years. Morgan Stanley estimates +3-4% EPS impact for FY26-27. A bold bet that demand is firming.

3. SKU Rationalization

Zodiac/aged/non-standard Moutai volumes to be cut 30-50%. Focus on core Feitian + premium >¥2,000 tier. Discontinuing speculative collector products.

4. Distributor Reform

New "consignment model" — 5% rebate after sale, giving Moutai direct channel data. Potential relaxation of distributor qualification (echoing 2013-14 playbook).

5. Capital Return Acceleration

FY24 payout ratio hit 75%. Proposed FY25 final dividend ¥27.99. Share buyback program continuing. Management explicitly using excess cash to support shareholder returns.

6. New Consumer Segmentation

Explicitly targeting "specialized and innovative" private enterprises, family gatherings, lifestyle scenarios — pivoting away from government/SOE banquets as the demand driver.

Signal reading / 信号解读

Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley both treat the March 2026 ex-factory price hike as a signal that the baijiu industry has bottomed. We are cautiously skeptical — "bottomed" calls from sell-side are frequently early — but we agree the strategic response is coherent and aggressive. If management executes, the base case becomes more likely than the bear case.

10. Final Verdict & Position Sizing · 最终结论与仓位建议

The Bottom Line · 核心结论

Kweichow Moutai is one of the highest-quality consumer businesses on earth. Its brand, cost structure, and balance sheet are all Buffett-grade. The FY2025 decline is real but mild — far from the catastrophe the "first-ever decline" headline suggests. The company is entering a multi-year transition from a speculative collectors' asset back to a consumer staple, which will likely compress growth to low single digits for several years but should stabilize the long-term franchise.

However, the stock is not screamingly cheap. At CNY 1,407, it trades at roughly fair value — an ~8% margin of safety against our probability-weighted intrinsic value of CNY 1,520. For a China A-share with policy overhang, that margin is too thin to back the truck up.
