Copper & Electricity: The Physical Layer of AI Infrastructure

铜与电力：AI基础设施的物理基石 · 黄仁勋 "电力是瓶颈，不是芯片" 延伸研究
Jensen Huang identified power — not chips — as the binding constraint on AI scaling. Every $50B AI factory needs ~1GW of 24/7 baseload power. Every GW of data center capacity requires thousands of tonnes of copper for wiring, busbars, switchgear, and power distribution. This report identifies 10 companies across US and HK markets positioned at these critical physical bottlenecks.
Research Date: April 7, 2026 · Copper hit record $14,000/tonne in Jan 2026
The Investment Thesis: Why Copper & Electricity Are the Ultimate AI Picks-and-Shovels
投资逻辑：为什么铜与电力是AI的终极 "卖铲人"

Power is the bottleneck. Huang stated explicitly that a 1GW data center campus cannot magically become 2GW — the metric that matters is "tokens per watt." US electricity demand is projected to grow ~2% annually through 2030, with AI data centers consuming 1.5% of global supply (equivalent to the entire UK) and doubling by 2030. Hyperscaler capex is expected to exceed $700 billion in 2026, with ~90% flowing to power-hungry AI infrastructure.

Copper is the wiring of the AI era. BloombergNEF projects data center copper demand reaching 572,000 tonnes annually by 2028. J.P. Morgan projects a refined copper shortfall of ~330,000 tonnes in 2026. Goldman Sachs calls copper the commodity with the "highest growth potential" in the AI and electrification supercycle. A single hyperscale AI data center consumes more copper than a traditional facility by orders of magnitude — busbars, switchgear, PDUs, and miles of high-amperage cabling.

The supply-demand gap is structural, not cyclical. WoodMac expects global copper demand to surge 24% by 2035, requiring $210 billion of new mining investment — vs. only $76 billion invested over the past six years. S&P Global warns copper supply could fall short of demand by 10 million tonnes by 2040. Meanwhile, new mines take 15-20 years from discovery to production.

Freeport is the world's purest large-cap copper play — 75% of revenue from copper, with world-class assets spanning Indonesia (Grasberg), Arizona (Morenci, Bagdad), Peru (Cerro Verde), and Chile (El Abra). The company is using AI to optimize ore sequencing, mill throughput, and recovery rates, targeting 300M additional pounds from leaching innovation in 2026. Amazon Web Services signed a landmark 2-year agreement to purchase domestically produced copper from an Arizona mine — the first direct link between low-carbon copper and AI infrastructure.

A 50% US tariff on copper imports (effective Aug 2025) gives domestic producers like FCX a structural pricing advantage, with COMEX trading at a 9%+ premium to LME. The Grasberg mud rush (Sep 2025) temporarily reduced output, with phased restart targeting ~85% capacity by H2 2026. FCX's project pipeline targets 2.5 billion additional pounds of annual copper plus 0.5M oz gold from Bagdad expansion, El Abra phase 2, and Kucing Liar.

Grasberg is one of the world's largest copper/gold deposits. Downstream integration in Indonesia (new smelter operational). AI-enhanced leaching is a proprietary technology advantage. US tariff creates pricing moat for domestic production.

Eaton is the "electrical backbone" company for every AI data center. Its data center backlog equals 11 years of what was built in 2025 — a staggering metric that CEO Paulo Ruiz Sternadt highlighted on the Q4 earnings call. Data center orders accelerated 200% in Q4. Eaton provides the critical power distribution infrastructure: switchgear, UPS systems, PDUs, transformers, and its proprietary busbar technology (rigid metal conductors that replace cables, reducing power loss in high-density AI racks).

This is the company that sits between the utility meter and the GPU rack — every watt of power flowing to NVIDIA's Blackwell systems passes through Eaton's electrical infrastructure. The company also produces liquid cooling distribution and software for power management optimization.

Moat

Category leader in electrical power distribution for data centers. 11-year backlog provides extraordinary visibility. Busbar technology is a differentiated product for high-density AI racks.
