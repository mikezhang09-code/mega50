Why Hong Kong? The China AI Thesis
为什么关注港股？中国AI投资逻辑

Jensen Huang's GTC 2026 thesis explicitly warned that China will build its own AI technology stack if American technology isn't available there — and that this parallel stack would eventually compete globally. The evidence is already materializing: DeepSeek's viral moment in 2025 catalyzed a massive revaluation of Chinese AI assets. Hong Kong's Q1 2026 IPO fundraising hit a 5-year high of ~$14 billion, driven almost entirely by AI companies.

The HK market offers exposure to themes that don't exist in the US list: domestic GPU self-sufficiency (SMIC, Biren), AI-native consumer platforms at Chinese scale (1.4B users via WeChat/Kuaishou), physical AI + EV convergence (Xiaomi's factory robots), and frontier LLM labs going public (Zhipu, MiniMax — China's "AI Tigers"). Tencent and Alibaba together plan to spend RMB 150B+ on AI in 2026, creating a domestic AI infrastructure cycle that mirrors but is independent from the US hyperscaler buildout.

Tencent is the most diversified AI play in China, uniquely positioned to embed AI into 1.3 billion WeChat users' daily lives. FY2025 results showed AI working as a performance multiplier across existing profit centers: marketing services grew 19% powered by AI ad targeting, domestic gaming revenue surged 18% (Delta Force, VALORANT Mobile), and Business Services accelerated to 22% growth in Q4 driven by cloud + AI workloads.

The company spent RMB 18 billion on AI products in 2025 and plans to double that in 2026. Management launched QClaw — an advanced AI agent within WeChat — and is preparing Hunyuan 3.0, its next-gen LLM, for April 2026. Tencent Cloud achieved profitability at scale for the first time. The strategic shift is from "building a unified AI entry point" to distributing agentic capabilities across the WeChat ecosystem — precisely the "every company needs an agentic strategy" thesis Huang described.

Competitive Moat 护城河

WeChat's 1.3B MAU ecosystem is an unassailable distribution moat. Closed-loop ad commerce (Mini Programs → Mini Shops) creates an AI monetization flywheel no competitor can replicate. International games revenue surpassed USD 10 billion.

Key Risks 主要风险

Management admitted AI development pace has lagged — the "All in AI" pivot is late relative to Alibaba and ByteDance. GPU supply constraints limited 2025 capex. Reduced buybacks in 2026 to fund AI investment may pressure short-term shareholder returns. Regulatory overhang persists.

Strong Buy — 45 of 52 analysts Avg Target: ~HK$580 (30%+ upside)

Xiaomi is the purest "Physical AI" play on the HK market — directly embodying Huang's thesis that the $50 trillion physical world is technology's next frontier. The Smart EV + AI segment hit RMB 106.1 billion revenue (+224% YoY) and achieved its first annual operating profit in just 19 months since launching the SU7. But this is far more than an EV story. Xiaomi launched MiMo-V2-Pro, ranking 8th globally and 2nd in China on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index. The open-sourced Xiaomi-Robotics-0 VLA model powers embodied robots that ran autonomously for 3 hours at Xiaomi's EV factory in March 2026, achieving 90.2% success rate on assembly tasks. Xiaomi's miclaw (their OpenClaw-based mobile agent) entered beta testing. R&D investment hit RMB 33.1 billion, with 60 billion planned for AI over three years.

Competitive Moat 护城河

"Human × Car × Home" ecosystem integrates 165M smartphone users with EV + AIoT devices. Manufacturing expertise from smartphone mass production transfers to vehicle production. Cost discipline: EV segment achieved 24.3% gross margin in year one — unprecedented for a new EV entrant.

EV competition in China is brutal — BYD, NIO, Huawei all competing. Stock has pulled back ~45% from highs. Smartphone ASP pressure from memory chip shortages. EV tax break phaseout could dampen demand. Robotics commercialization timeline uncertain.

SMIC is China's TSMC equivalent — the foundry backbone of domestic AI chip production. As US export controls block Chinese companies from accessing TSMC's advanced nodes, SMIC becomes the critical enabler for every domestic GPU maker (Biren, Moore Threads, MetaX, Iluvatar). FY2025 revenue rose to $9.3 billion with net profit surging 39%, driven by AI chip demand that is "squeezing out" orders from lower-end smartphone and consumer electronics customers.

SMIC operates at mature nodes (primarily 28nm and above), but has demonstrated the ability to produce 7nm-class chips using multi-patterning on older equipment — a remarkable engineering achievement under sanctions. The company shipped 9.7 million wafers in 2025, up 21% YoY, and maintains massive capex (~$7B annually) to expand capacity.

Competitive Moat 护城河

Irreplaceable strategic position as China's most advanced foundry. Government policy support guarantees demand. Capacity expansion creates long-term scale advantages. Every Chinese AI chip startup MUST fab through SMIC or Hua Hong.

Key Risks 主要风险

US sanctions limit access to EUV lithography, capping node advancement. Gross margins (22%) significantly below TSMC (55%+). Revenue guidance for Q1 2026 was flat. Entity List restrictions create ongoing uncertainty. Low profitability relative to global peers.

Buy — 17 of 22 analysts Avg Target: HK$77 (~50% upside from ~HK$51)
