The Greenwald Neglect Screen

A systematic framework for finding structurally under-researched companies across US, Hong Kong, China, and India markets — then validating them through Graham-Buffett quality and valuation filters. 00
The Greenwald Neglect Thesis
格林沃尔德的 "被忽视价值" 理论
"The best opportunities are found not in the securities everyone is analyzing, but in the ones no one is looking at."
— Bruce Greenwald, Value Investing: From Graham to Buffett and Beyond

Greenwald's insight is deceptively simple: market efficiency is a function of attention. Where dozens of analysts, quant funds, and algorithmic traders are dissecting every earnings whisper, prices tend to be tight. Move to the parts of the market where nobody is paying attention — and you find genuine mispricings that your analytical work can exploit.

This isn't just a small-cap bias. It's a structural information advantage thesis. The mechanism works through a clear chain: low analyst coverage → low institutional ownership → thin liquidity → prices that can drift far from intrinsic value. Your job is to fish in those waters, apply rigorous Graham-Buffett analysis, and wait for the market to re-discover what you already know.

Why It Works

The Attention Economy of Markets 市场的注意力经济

Analyst coverage, media attention, and institutional mandates are finite resources. They concentrate on large, liquid, index-member stocks. Everything outside that spotlight is structurally under-priced for information quality — creating opportunity for the patient, independent analyst.

Key Distinction

Neglected ≠ Bad 被忽视 ≠ 坏公司

The critical insight: being ignored by analysts is not the same as being a poor business. Many neglected companies are profitable, well-managed, and durable — they simply lack the market cap, glamour, or narrative to attract Wall Street's attention. That's where the alpha lives.

Integration Point

Greenwald × Graham-Buffett 格林沃尔德 × 格雷厄姆-巴菲特

Greenwald tells you where to look. Graham-Buffett tells you what to buy. This framework uses Greenwald's neglect thesis as a first-pass universe filter, then applies rigorous value and quality analysis to the resulting candidates.

The two-stage logic: Stage 1 asks "Is this company structurally under-researched?" — using measurable proxies for analyst neglect. Stage 2 asks "Is it actually a good business at a good price?" — using our full Graham-Buffett toolkit. Only companies that pass both stages make the watchlist. This prevents two common errors: buying well-covered stocks where you have no edge, and buying neglected junk just because it's cheap.

01
Stage 1 — The Neglect Filter
第一阶段 — 忽视过滤器

This stage identifies companies that are structurally under-researched by the market. Each criterion below is a measurable proxy for "the market isn't paying attention." A company doesn't need to pass every one — the more it passes, the higher its Neglect Score, and the more likely your analytical work will produce an informational edge.

ID Neglect Signal / 忽视信号 Threshold Points Rationale
N1 Low Analyst Coverage
分析师覆盖少		 ≤ 3 sell-side analysts 3 The single strongest neglect signal. Directly measures how many professionals are watching. Most S&P 500 names have 20–30; your targets should have ≤ 3, ideally 0–1.
N2 Low Institutional Ownership
机构持股比例低		 < 30% institutional holding 2 Low institutional ownership means fewer sophisticated eyes on the stock, and less efficient pricing. Also means less passive index-driven buying/selling.
N3 Low Media Mentions
媒体提及少		 < 5 articles/month in major financial press 1 Media coverage drives retail attention and narrative momentum. Absence of coverage = absence of herd behavior.
N4 Not in Major Index
非主要指数成分		 Not in S&P 500 / HSI / Nifty 50 / CSI 300 2 Index membership forces passive funds to own a stock, creates analyst coverage mandates, and guarantees liquidity. Non-members avoid all of this.
N5 Small/Mid Cap
中小盘股		 Market cap < $5B (US) / HK$40B (HK) / ₹40,000 Cr (India) 2 Most institutional mandates have minimum market-cap thresholds. Below them, you're fishing where the big boats can't go.
N6 "Boring" Industry
"无聊"行业		 Industrial, niche consumer, specialty chemical, regional utility, etc. 1 Glamour bias: analysts and funds flock to AI, crypto, biotech. Companies making fasteners, niche packaging, or regional insurance get structurally ignored.
N7 Situational Neglect
特殊情境导致的忽视		 Recent spinoff, post-crisis, lost coverage, sector rotation away, delisted from index 2 Temporary events that cause attention withdrawal. Spinoffs are classic — parent company analysts don't cover the new entity, creating a coverage orphan.
N8 Language/Listing Barrier
语言/上市壁垒		 Filings primarily in non-English; listed in market with low foreign participation 2 Especially powerful in HK/China. English-language coverage of mid-cap HKEx stocks is often near zero. If you can read Chinese filings, your edge is structural.

Neglect Score Interpretation

忽视评分解读
Neglect Score = sum of all N-criteria points met (max 15)

12–15 points → Deeply neglected — maximum information edge potential
8–11 points → Moderately neglected — solid edge, proceed to Stage 2
5–7 points → Lightly neglected — edge exists but narrower
< 5 points → Well-covered — little neglect-based edge, screen out

Minimum threshold to advance: Neglect Score ≥ 5. Below this, the company is too well-covered to offer a structural informational advantage.

02
Stage 2 — Quality Gate
第二阶段 — 质量关口

Companies that pass Stage 1 are structurally neglected — but neglect alone isn't a reason to buy. Stage 2 applies a streamlined version of our Graham-Buffett quality screen. The goal is to eliminate junk — low-quality businesses that are cheap for good reason — while allowing genuinely good companies to pass through to the valuation stage.

"Price is what you pay; value is what you get. But first, make sure you're getting something worth having."
— Adapted from Warren Buffett
ID Quality Gate / 质量关口 Threshold Weight Kill?
Q1 Profitability 盈利能力 Positive net income ≥ 3 of last 5 years Required KILL
Q2 Cash Generation 现金生成 Positive operating cash flow ≥ 4 of last 5 years Required KILL
Q3 Balance Sheet Safety 资产负债表安全 Debt/Equity < 1.5 and Current Ratio > 1.2 Required KILL
Q4 Return on Capital 资本回报率 ROIC > 8% average over last 5 years Preferred —
Q5 Revenue Stability 收入稳定性 Revenue declined > 25% YoY no more than once in 5 years Preferred —
Q6 Insider Alignment 管理层利益一致 Management owns > 5% OR significant recent insider buying Bonus —
Q7 Audit Quality 审计质量 Big 4 auditor (especially critical for HK/China names) Bonus —
Q8 FCF Conversion 自由现金流转化 FCF / Net Income > 70% average Preferred —

Kill criteria (Q1–Q3) are non-negotiable. If a company fails any of these, it's out — no exceptions. These protect you from value traps: companies that look cheap on P/E or P/B but are cheap because the business is deteriorating. Preferred criteria (Q4, Q5, Q8) indicate quality but aren't absolute requirements. Bonus criteria (Q6, Q7) add conviction but their absence isn't disqualifying.

Value Trap Protection

Why Q1–Q3 Are Kill Gates 为何Q1–Q3是淘汰关

Neglected stocks are disproportionately likely to include value traps — companies that are cheap and neglected because they deserve to be. The kill criteria ensure we only advance fundamentally sound businesses. The Greenwald edge comes from finding quality in neglect, not from buying distressed names blindly.

Flexibility by Market

Adjusting for HK/China/India 针对港/中/印的调整

For HK/China: tighten Q3 (require Current Ratio > 2.0, D/E < 1.0) and make Q7 (Big 4 audit) a required gate. For India: pay extra attention to Q6 — family conglomerates with low public governance need higher insider alignment evidence. For US small-caps: standard thresholds apply.

03
Stage 3 — Valuation & Margin of Safety
第三阶段 — 估值与安全边际

Companies that survive Stages 1 and 2 are neglected, quality businesses. Now we ask the final question: is the price right? This stage applies the full Graham-Buffett valuation toolkit, with a critical Greenwald adjustment — neglected stocks deserve a higher margin of safety requirement because their thin liquidity means you may not be able to exit quickly if your thesis is wrong.

ID Valuation Metric / 估值指标 Target Range Notes
V1 P/E Ratio 市盈率 < 12× (vs. Graham's standard 15×) Tighter than normal because neglected = illiquid = needs wider buffer
V2 P/B Ratio 市净率 < 1.5× (or P/E × P/B < 22.5) Graham's classic combined test. For net-net candidates, require P/B < 0.8×
V3 EV/EBIT 企业价值/息税前利润 < 8× (vs. standard < 10×) Captures the full capital structure. Especially useful for comparing across leveraged vs. unleveraged companies.
V4 FCF Yield 自由现金流收益率 > 8% (vs. standard > 5%) Higher bar because your holding period may be longer (catalyst uncertainty in neglected names)
V5 Margin of Safety 安全边际 > 30% below intrinsic value estimate Use owner earnings or EPV-based intrinsic value. Add market-specific MoS adjustments (HK +10%, China ADR +15%, India mid-cap +15%)
V6 Dividend Yield 股息率 > 3% (if applicable) Not required for all candidates, but for neglected names, dividends provide "paid to wait" comfort. Strong signal of cash-flow reality.

The Greenwald Valuation Adjustment

格林沃尔德的估值调整
Required MoS = Base MoS + Neglect Premium + Market Premium

Base MoS: 20% (standard Graham)
Neglect Premium: +5% if Neglect Score 8-11, +10% if Score 12-15
Market Premium: per cross-market table (HK +5–10%, China +10–20%, India +5–15%)

Example: A deeply neglected (Score 13) HK small-cap requires 20% + 10% + 10% = 40% MoS

This higher bar reflects two realities: (1) your valuation of a neglected company has wider uncertainty bands because less public information exists, and (2) exit liquidity is thinner, so you need more cushion. It's Graham's original logic — MoS protects against both analytical error and bad luck — applied more aggressively to an inherently uncertain domain.

🇺🇸 US Playbook

美国攻略

The US market is the most efficient of the four, so neglect-based edges are narrower but still real. Focus on micro-caps ($50M–$500M), companies recently dropped from the Russell 2000, spinoffs within the first 6 months of independent trading, and family-controlled industrials in unsexy sectors. Data quality is excellent — SEC filings, EDGAR, proxy statements — so the analytical work is about interpreting existing data that nobody else is reading.

Key signals: 0–2 analysts covering · not in Russell 2000 · market cap < $500M · insider buying > 1% of float in last 6 months · boring SIC code (industrial machinery, specialty chemicals, regional distribution) · recent spinoff or index deletion.

Traps to avoid: Pink sheet / OTC stocks with poor disclosure · micro-caps with no operating history · special acquisition vehicles (SPACs) masquerading as operating companies.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong Playbook

香港攻略

HK is the richest neglect hunting ground globally. Over 2,600 companies listed on HKEx, but institutional coverage concentrates on the ~80 HSI/HSCEI components. Everything else is a vast, under-researched universe. English-language coverage of non-blue-chip HK stocks is near zero. The combination of language barrier + small float + China-adjacent risk perception creates deep, persistent mispricings.

Key signals: 0 English-language analyst coverage · not in HSI or HSCEI · market cap HK$2B–HK$30B · listed > 5 years with consistent dividend · Big 4 auditor · no VIE structure (prefer direct operating subsidiaries) · available in Mandarin/Cantonese filings only.

Traps to avoid: Shell companies and financial engineering vehicles · aggressive related-party transactions · companies with frequent share dilution · non-Big 4 auditors in mainland subsidiaries · companies with <3 year listing history.

🇨🇳 China A-Share Playbook

中国A股攻略

A-shares present a paradox: the market is retail-dominated, meaning prices are often sentiment-driven and disconnected from fundamentals, but accounting quality is lower, making fundamental analysis harder. The neglect edge here comes from applying rigorous Western-style value analysis to companies that Chinese retail investors trade on charts and tips. Non-CSI 300 companies with solid fundamentals and low institutional ownership are prime territory.

Key signals: Not in CSI 300 or CSI 500 · low QFII/Stock Connect holding · high retail ownership · consistent dividend payer · SOE with asset revaluation potential · provincial champion in niche industry.

Traps to avoid: Accounting irregularities (verify FCF/NI > 70%) · ST/PT designated stocks · companies with large "other receivables" (often related-party loans) · local government financing platforms disguised as operating companies.

🇮🇳 India Playbook

印度攻略

India's BSE lists 5,000+ companies, but meaningful coverage extends to perhaps 300–400. The BSE SmallCap index alone has ~750 companies, most with minimal or zero institutional research. The domestic economic growth story (demographics, digitization, formalization) means many of these neglected small-caps are riding genuine long-term tailwinds — they're just too small for FIIs to notice. Focus on companies benefiting from India's infrastructure buildout, consumption growth, and IT services expansion.

Key signals: Not in Nifty 50 or Nifty Next 50 · low FII ownership · market cap ₹500–₹10,000 Cr · consistent ROE > 12% · promoter holding > 50% with good governance track record · niche domestic market leader.

Traps to avoid: Promoter pledging (> 20% of holding pledged) · frequent related-party transactions · companies with complex cross-holding structures · shell companies in BSE micro-cap segment · accounting red flags (large intangibles, frequent goodwill write-downs).

Composite Greenwald-Graham-Buffett Score
综合评分体系

The final ranking system integrates all three stages into a single composite score. This isn't a replacement for deep analysis — it's a triage tool that tells you which candidates deserve your full 7-step value investing analysis first.

Composite Score Formula

综合评分公式
GGB Score = (Neglect Score / 15 × 30) + (Quality Pass Rate × 30) + (Valuation Score / 6 × 40)

Neglect Score: from Stage 1 (max 15 pts, weighted 30%)
Quality Pass Rate: # of Q-criteria met / 8 (weighted 30%)
Valuation Score: # of V-criteria met / 6 (weighted 40%)

Maximum possible: 100 points
GGB Score Classification / 分类 Action
80–100 Prime Neglect Candidate 优质忽视标的 Immediately run full 7-step Graham-Buffett analysis. Highest priority for capital allocation. These are the rare gems — genuinely good businesses that the market has forgotten about.
60–79 Strong Candidate 优选标的 Queue for full analysis. Likely passes on most dimensions with one area slightly weak (e.g., great neglect + quality but valuation only fair). May need a catalyst or patience.
40–59 Watchlist 观察名单 Monitor for improvement. Usually the valuation isn't compelling enough yet, or quality is borderline. Set price alerts and re-screen quarterly.
< 40 Pass 放弃 Does not meet the framework's threshold. Either not neglected enough (well-covered), not high enough quality (value trap risk), or not cheap enough (no margin of safety).
Weighting Rationale

Why 30/30/40? 为何是30/30/40?

Valuation gets the highest weight (40%) because ultimately, price determines returns — Graham's cardinal rule. Neglect and quality share equal weight (30% each) because you need both: neglect without quality is a junk trap, quality without neglect means no informational edge. The 30% neglect weight ensures we don't drift back to well-covered stocks just because they score well on quality and valuation.

Using the Score

Triage, Not Gospel 分诊工具，非圣经

The GGB score decides what to analyze first, not what to buy. Even a 90-score candidate needs the full 7-step deep dive before capital allocation. Conversely, a 55-score company might become a 75 after a price drop or earnings improvement. Screen quarterly, re-rank, and let the framework guide your research agenda.

08
Screening Cadence & Workflow
筛选节奏与工作流
1

Quarterly Universe Scan 季度全面扫描

Run the full Stage 1 neglect filter across all four markets. Generate a raw list of 50–100 neglected candidates. Use screener tools (Finviz, AASTOCKS, Screener.in, East Money) with analyst count and market cap filters.

2

Quality Triage (1–2 Days) 质量分诊 (1-2天)

Apply Stage 2 kill criteria (Q1–Q3) to the raw list. This should eliminate 60–70% of candidates. Quick-scan balance sheets and income statements — don't do deep dives yet.

3

Valuation Screen (1–2 Days) 估值筛选 (1-2天)

For survivors, pull valuation ratios (P/E, P/B, EV/EBIT, FCF yield). Compute GGB Composite Score. Rank the top 10–15 candidates by score.

4

Deep Dive (Ongoing) 深度研究 (持续)

Take the top 3–5 ranked candidates and run the full 7-step Graham-Buffett analysis. This is where you read the annual report, analyze the moat, compute intrinsic value via multiple methods, and formulate a thesis. Target: 1 deep dive per week.

5

Catalyst Watch 催化剂观察

For watchlist names (GGB 40–59), set price alerts at your target entry zone and monitor for catalysts: earnings surprises, new analyst initiations, index inclusion rumors, insider buying spikes, or sector rotation. A catalyst can rapidly convert a neglected stock into a recognized one.
