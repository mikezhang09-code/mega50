The Four-Stage Funnel (v1.1)四阶段漏斗

This screen executes the Greenwald Neglect × Graham-Buffett (GGB) v1.1 framework. Each stage has explicit pass/fail gates; candidates that fail at any stage are eliminated from advancement.

STAGE 1: Neglect Filter (N1–N8, max 15 pts) → Gate: ≥ 5/15 STAGE 1.5: Quick Verify (N1 + 3 deal-breaker checks) → Gate: confirmed neglect, no M&A/profit warning/going concern STAGE 2: Quality Gate (Q1–Q10) → Gate: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q9 ALL pass STAGE 3: Valuation & MoS (V1–V6, max 6 pts) → Rank by GGB Composite

GGB Composite Score FormulaGGB 综合评分公式

GGB Score = (Neglect / 15 × 25) + (Quality_Pass_Rate × 25) + (G-Score / 10 × 20) + (Valuation / 6 × 30) Maximum: 100 points

The v1.1 formula explicitly weights governance at 20% (up from 15% in v1.0). The inaugural HK screen showed governance was the primary differentiator between "invest" and "don't invest" decisions; explicit weighting prevents a great-value/great-neglect/terrible-governance trap from scoring incorrectly high.

US-Specific Margin of Safety Adjustment美国市场专属安全边际调整

Per the market playbooks reference, US small-caps require a higher MoS than US large-caps. For this screen:

US small-cap, Neglect 5–7 → 30% required MoS US small-cap, Neglect 8–11 → 30% required MoS US micro-cap, Neglect 12+ → 35% required MoS

All three surviving candidates fall in the 8–11 neglect band; the 30% MoS is therefore the threshold against which V5 was scored.

Data Sourcing Hierarchy (Tier 1/2/3)数据来源层级

Tier 3 (screening-only, never for scoring): Seeking Alpha headlines, TipRanks blurbs, Substack research notes. Used for candidate discovery; never as primary basis for any score.

Why the US Screen Returns Few Names为什么美国筛选返回的名字很少

This is the most efficient market in the GGB universe. Per the US playbook:

  • Russell 2000 already pulls in names down to ~$120M cap, providing institutional scrutiny
  • Aggregator services (Yahoo, Simply Wall St) cover essentially every SEC-registered name
  • Genuine N1 ≤ 3 neglect concentrated in micro-caps, OTC names (often trapped), or family-controlled industrials too small for index inclusion
  • The "neglect premium" in US is real but narrow — single-digit basis points of edge

The next screen of the same universe should target HK/China/India for richer hunting; consult market-playbooks.md for those market-specific signals.
