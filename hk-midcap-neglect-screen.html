Stage 1.5 Quick Verify Eliminations · 17 of 28 Killed Of 28 names that passed the raw Neglect Score ≥ 5 threshold at Stage 1, 17 were eliminated at Stage 1.5 — a 61% kill rate, very close to the 63% false-positive rate found in the inaugural screen. Reasons: (a) 9 names had confirmed analyst count > 5 once verified across AASTOCKS + a second source (the SSY Group / 2005.HK pattern); (b) 3 names had material ownership-change events in last 12 months (the Canvest / 1381.HK pattern); (c) 3 names had recent dilutive placements > 10% (the Time Interconnect / 1729.HK pattern); (d) 2 names issued profit warnings of > 50% YoY decline. Of the 11 that survived Stage 1.5, 4 more were eliminated at Stage 2 quality gating (Q1/Q2/Q3 KILL failures), leaving the 7 that appear in the table. This validates that Stage 1.5 is doing exactly what it was designed to do — preventing wasted deep-dive cycles.
Verify-Pending Candidates · Why They're Flagged Three candidates in the table (1212.HK Lifestyle, 0341.HK Café de Coral, 0668.HK Daido) carry ⚠ VERIFY badges. Tier 2 sources (AASTOCKS, Simply Wall St, Stockopedia) suggest these names likely meet Stage 1 and Stage 2 thresholds — and Lifestyle and Café de Coral compute provisional GGB scores in Strong territory (65.0 and 61.2 respectively). However, verdict-moving Q1–Q3 kill criteria have not been confirmed from primary HKEx filings. Per v1.1 rules, these names cannot advance to Step 7 deep dive (using value-investing skill) until: N1 confirmed from ≥2 independent sources; Q1–Q3 verified from audited statements; Q9 (going concern) and Q10 (non-core discipline) checked from auditor's report and segment notes. Provisional GGB scores are upper bounds — if verification fails any KILL gate, the candidate is eliminated regardless of provisional score.
Carry-Forward Candidates · Updated Scoring The two confirmed Strong Candidates from the April 2026 inaugural screen — Dah Sing Financial (0440.HK) and Kinetic Development (1277.HK) — are re-scored here with one quarter of additional data. Both retain their Strong classification. Dah Sing's score moved from 69.7 → 72.2 (+2.5) on confirmed FY2025 H1 banking subsidiary results — NIM expanded 24bps to 2.41% and banking profit grew 20% YoY, lifting Q4 (ROIC trajectory) and Q5 (revenue stability) within the quality component. Kinetic's score moved from 69.2 → 67.2 (−2.0) as FY2025 H1 non-core losses widened on a smaller core profit base, increasing the G8 deduction from −1 to −2. Neither is yet a Prime candidate; both warrant continued monitoring of their respective gating issues — Dah Sing: ISS governance and HK commercial real estate exposure; Kinetic: going-concern resolution and non-core discipline.
