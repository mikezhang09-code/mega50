🔴 Critical Observation: February Milestone

For the first time in BYD's history, overseas sales exceeded domestic sales in February 2026 (52.9% export ratio). This is both a testament to international expansion success AND a red flag for collapsing domestic demand.

Context: Q1 2026 total sales down 30% YoY vs. Q1 2025, marking the seventh consecutive month of YoY declines.

The Great Divergence: Domestic vs. Overseas 国内与海外市场分化

❌ Domestic Market Collapse

  • Brutal price war: Margins compressed from 19.4% to 17.7% gross margin
  • Policy headwinds: 5% NEV purchase tax introduced Jan 2026 (first tax since 2014)
  • Market saturation: NEV penetration approaching 60% in major cities
  • Fierce competition: Geely, Xiaomi, Leapmotor gaining share in mass market
  • Tech gap closing: BYD's Blade Battery and DM-i hybrid advantages eroding
  • Jan-Feb 2026: Total sales plunged 36% YoY; Geely temporarily overtook BYD as domestic leader

✅ Overseas Momentum Strong

  • Volume surge: 1.05M exports in 2025 (+151% YoY), targeting 1.3-1.5M in 2026
  • Higher margins: 19.5% gross margin overseas vs ~14% domestic (est.)
  • Premium pricing: Models in Germany, Brazil, Australia priced 20-30% above China
  • Geographic diversification: Europe, Latin America, SE Asia, Middle East expansion
  • Localization strategy: Factories in Hungary, Brazil, Thailand mitigate tariff risks
  • Q1 2026 exports: 321K units, maintaining ~100-120K/month run rate

📊 Key Insight: R&D Exceeds Profit

BYD spent RMB 63.4B on R&D in FY2025, nearly 2x its net profit (RMB 32.6B). Cumulative R&D investment has exceeded RMB 240B since inception. This "profits-for-tech-moat" trade-off is deliberate strategic positioning for long-term leadership, but puts near-term profitability under severe pressure.

Investment Thesis Reassessment 投资论点重估

✅ Bull Case Remains Valid

  • Global EV crown: Overtook Tesla as world's largest BEV seller (2.26M vs 1.64M)
  • Vertical integration moat: Only major automaker controlling entire supply chain from batteries to semiconductors
  • Overseas growth engine: 151% YoY export growth demonstrates global competitiveness
  • Technology leadership: Blade Battery 2.0 with 9-minute full charge, DM-i hybrid 2,100km range
  • Premium brand scaling: Denza, Yangwang, Fangchengbao combined sales doubled (though still <5% of mix)
  • Infrastructure buildout: 20,000 flash charging stations target creates ecosystem lock-in
  • Financial fortress: RMB 156B cash, minimal debt, FCF positive despite heavy R&D

❌ Bear Case Strengthened

  • Profitability freefall: Net profit down 19% despite 3.5% revenue growth — margin compression worse than feared
  • Domestic market share erosion: Geely overtook BYD in Jan-Feb 2026; Xiaomi YU7 outselling BYD models
  • Technology differentiation closing: Competitors catching up on battery tech, smart cabin, ADAS
  • Execution risk on overseas: Hungary factory delayed, localization costs rising, tariff headwinds in EU/US
  • Inventory overhang: Elevated inventory turnover days (70-80 vs industry 30-45) signals demand weakness
  • Capital intensity rising: Overseas factory buildouts require massive capex with uncertain ROI
  • Subsidy cliff: China NEV purchase tax returning puts structural pressure on demand
