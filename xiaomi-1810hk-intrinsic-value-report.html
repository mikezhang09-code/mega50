Investment Thesis
投资论点

Xiaomi trades at its 52-week low of HKD 30.88 — roughly half its June 2025 peak — creating the most compelling entry point since 2022 for a company generating RMB 39.2B in adjusted profit with a rapidly scaling EV business. The probability-weighted intrinsic value estimate of HKD 39.35 implies approximately 27% upside.

The transformation from a smartphone discounter into a diversified hardware-internet-automotive conglomerate is real: the EV segment went from zero revenue to RMB 103.3B in 20 months while turning profitable. Internet services at 76.5% gross margin provide a high-quality annuity stream built atop 754M MAU and 1.07B connected IoT devices.

However, the stock's discount reflects genuine risks: a collapsing India business (₹4,820Cr frozen), smartphone segment stagnation amid memory cost inflation, and a brutally competitive China EV price war. The 21.5% margin of safety falls short of the 33% Graham purists demand, making this a conditional BUY at 2–4% portfolio weight for investors comfortable with China tech risk.

EV Business — The Thesis Driver
电动车 — 核心投资逻辑

In just 20 months from first delivery, Xiaomi achieved what most automakers take a decade: 411,082 vehicles delivered generating RMB 103.3B at 24.3% gross margin, transforming from RMB 6.2B operating loss (FY2024) to RMB 0.9B profit (FY2025).

The SU7 outsold Tesla Model 3 in China by 29%. Monthly deliveries scaled from ~20K to 50K+ through 2025. The 2026 target is 550K units with four new models launching.

EV gross margin expanded from 12.5% at launch to 25.5% in Q3 2025. ASP rose to RMB 251,200, with the SU7 Ultra at RMB 529,900 pushing into the premium tier.

Internet Services — The Profit Engine
互联网服务 — 利润引擎

At 76.5% gross margin, internet services remain the underappreciated gem. With 754M global MAU generating rising ARPU through advertising (RMB 28.5B, +15.2%), gaming, and fintech, this segment effectively monetizes the hardware installed base.

Overseas internet revenue grew ~18–20% and now represents 35% of the segment, reducing China concentration risk. The flywheel is clear: more devices → more users → more ARPU → more profit.

Competitive Landscape Summary
竞争格局总结

Smartphones: Xiaomi retains #3 globally (13.1%) but lost ground to Apple (20%) and Samsung (19.1%). Huawei's resurgence to China's #1 (16.4% share, up from #6 in 2023) is the biggest competitive threat domestically. Memory cost inflation and IDC's forecast of 12.9% decline in 2026 global shipments create near-term headwinds.

Electric Vehicles: BYD dominates with ~30% of China's NEV market. Xiaomi ranked #7 with 3.1% share but posted the highest growth rate (+195% YoY) among the top ten. The 5% NEV purchase tax in 2026 will test demand resilience across the sector.

IoT: The 1.07B-device ecosystem is Xiaomi's most defensible asset. No competitor matches the breadth — from phones to wearables, air conditioners to robot vacuums — under one software platform (HyperOS). This is the moat that matters most long-term.
